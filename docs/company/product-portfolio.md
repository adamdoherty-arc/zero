# Product Portfolio

The company product portfolio is one system with a flagship product and two
platform engines.

## Ada

Ada is the flagship commercial path.

| Layer | Personal Ada | Company Ada |
|---|---|---|
| Positioning | Adam's financial intelligence advisor | Financial decision-support product |
| Execution | Paper-default; live actions require approval | No autonomous live trading until compliance review |
| Core workflows | Portfolio health, CSP/wheel research, alerts, market context, paper trading | Explainable recommendations, risk evidence, monitoring, workflow automation |
| Safety | `financial` tier, explicit confirmation, audit trail | Compliance-reviewed claims, human approval, defensible logs |

Near-term product promise: Ada helps a user understand what is happening in a
portfolio, which opportunities deserve attention, why a trade idea is risky or
reasonable, and what evidence changed since the last review.

## Zero

Zero is the personal operating layer and second-brain interface. For the company,
Zero proves the memory, voice, and human-context loop that can later make Ada and
other agents feel continuous instead of stateless.

Zero is not a public financial product. It should not make trading decisions.

## Legion

Legion is the continuous engineering and operations layer. For the company,
Legion keeps code, docs, dependencies, model routes, tests, and audit queues
current. It is the system that lets the company learn without relying only on
human memory.

Legion is not a trader and does not originate financial actions.

## Shared Platform

Shared infra is a company asset:

- LiteLLM gateway and routing.
- Local Qwen3.6 chat and embedding models.
- Cloud fallbacks where policy allows.
- MCP fabric across Zero, Legion, Ada, vault, Postgres, browser, and future tools.
- Audit logs, approval tiers, memory stores, and runtime health.

## Product Boundaries

1. Ada can become a company product.
2. Zero and Legion can become internal platform leverage before they become
   separate products.
3. No public docs should claim autonomous live trading, registered-advisor
   status, or guaranteed outcomes.
4. The company should sell trust, clarity, and workflow leverage before it sells
   autonomy.
