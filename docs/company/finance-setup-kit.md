# ADA AI LLC Finance Setup Kit

This is an internal execution kit, not legal, tax, accounting, or credit advice.
Adam must personally approve filings, bank applications, credit applications,
legal documents, tax positions, and signed IP or asset transfers.

## Operating Stance

- Entity: ADA AI LLC, Florida single-member LLC.
- Federal tax baseline: disregarded entity unless CPA changes it.
- Year 1 election stance: no S-Corp election until durable profit justifies
  payroll, added filings, and reasonable-compensation work.
- Books: separate business records from the first ADA transaction forward.
- Deduction stance: ordinary, necessary, documented, and CPA-reviewed.
- Asset stance: existing personal property enters ADA only at documented fair
  market value (FMV), never at an unsupported markup.
- IP stance: ADA-related robot-control software and code should be assigned or
  licensed to ADA by written schedule, with attorney and CPA review.

## Step 1 - EIN

1. Search ADA records for an existing EIN confirmation letter before applying.
2. If none exists, apply directly through the IRS EIN assistant. The IRS says
   an EIN can be obtained directly from the IRS for free.
3. Save the CP 575 / EIN confirmation letter in secure company records.
4. Record:
   - legal name exactly as issued;
   - EIN issue date;
   - responsible party;
   - entity type;
   - secure document location;
   - who applied and when.

Do not use paid EIN resellers or enter identity data into third-party EIN sites.

## Step 2 - Business Bank Account

Open checking only after the EIN is confirmed. The SBA notes that a business
bank account can be opened once the federal EIN is obtained.

Prepare this packet:

| Item | Status |
|---|---|
| EIN confirmation letter | Pending |
| Florida Articles of Organization | Ready |
| Operating agreement | Pending signature |
| Ownership / sole-member statement | Pending |
| Government ID | Adam |
| Principal business address | Confirm |
| Initial owner contribution amount | Decide |
| Local license / Duval LBTR if requested | Pending |

First deposit should be an owner contribution, not revenue, so the opening
balance is cleanly classified.

## Step 3 - Business Credit Card

Priority: minimize personal-credit impact rather than maximize rewards.

Before applying, document each issuer's answer to:

| Policy | Decision Needed |
|---|---|
| Personal guarantee required? | Yes / no / terms |
| Personal hard inquiry likely? | Yes / no / bureau |
| Normal account activity reports to consumer bureaus? | Yes / no / only delinquency |
| Delinquency or default reports personally? | Yes / no |
| Annual fee, APR, late fee | Amount |
| Autopay support | Statement balance enabled |
| Employee card support | Optional |

Expect most small-business card issuers to require a personal guarantee and
often a personal hard inquiry. Normal activity may or may not report personally
depending on issuer; late or defaulted debt can affect Adam personally.

## Step 4 - Books And Receipt Intake

Create separate books before the first ADA-paid purchase. Minimum chart of
accounts:

| Account / Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| AI/API spend | OpenAI API, Anthropic, Gemini, model router, evals |
| Cloud and hosting | VPS, storage, observability, domains, DNS |
| Software tools | GitHub, IDEs, automation tools, SaaS |
| Hardware and equipment | Computers, monitors, components, robot |
| Office and home-office support | Desk, chair, business internet allocation |
| Professional services | CPA, attorney, registered agent |
| Dues and subscriptions | Business memberships, paid communities |
| Owner contributions | Money or property contributed by Adam |
| Owner reimbursements | CPA-approved reimbursements paid by ADA |
| Intangibles / software | Acquired or assigned software/IP value |

Each transaction needs vendor, date, amount, paid-from account, business
purpose, receipt link, category, and whether CPA review is required.

## Step 5 - Home Office Evidence

Collect evidence now; CPA chooses simplified vs actual method at tax time.
IRS Publication 587 allows a simplified method election yearly, but the space
must still qualify for business use.

Home-office worksheet inputs:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Exclusive business area square feet | Measure and photograph |
| Total home square feet | Source from lease, appraisal, or measurement |
| Months used for business | Start date matters |
| Rent or mortgage interest | Keep statements |
| Utilities | Electric, water, gas |
| Internet | Track business-use allocation |
| Insurance | Renters/homeowners |
| Repairs | Direct vs indirect |
| Direct office expenses | Paint, fixtures, office-only repairs |

## Step 6 - Existing Computers And Equipment

Build the asset register before any transfer:

| Evidence | Required Detail |
|---|---|
| Asset description | Make, model, configuration |
| Serial number | Photo if available |
| Photos | Front/back, labels, upgrades |
| Original receipt | If available |
| Upgrade receipts | GPU, RAM, storage, peripherals |
| Business-use percent | Good-faith estimate |
| Placed-in-service date | Date ADA starts using it |
| FMV evidence | Comparable listings, appraisals, resale data |
| Transfer method | Owner contribution or CPA-approved purchase/reimbursement |
| Memo | Signed contribution memo or bill of sale |

Upgrades and configuration can support FMV only if market comparables support
it. Do not invent a markup to create a deduction. IRS Publication 551 says not
to include the value of your own unpaid labor in the basis of property you
construct.

## Step 7 - Robot And Robot Software

If ADA buys the robot after banking is live:

- pay from the ADA checking account or business card;
- save invoice, warranty, serial, photos, and shipping proof;
- record business purpose, location, custodian, placed-in-service date, and
  expected use.

If the robot is already personally owned:

- transfer it like other equipment at documented FMV;
- use an owner contribution memo or bill of sale;
- attach photos, serial, purchase receipt, and comparable FMV evidence;
- ask CPA whether and how the transfer creates depreciable basis.

For robot-control software and ADA-related code:

- create an IP assignment or license schedule;
- list repositories, modules, trade names, documentation, and dependencies;
- state whether ADA receives ownership or a license;
- treat value conservatively because unpaid development time is not basis;
- ask CPA whether any software/intangible value is amortized, expensed, or
  treated as an owner contribution;
- ask attorney to review assignment scope and open-source/license obligations.

## Step 8 - BOI / Beneficial Ownership Info

FinCEN's current FAQ says entities created in the United States, including
entities formerly called domestic reporting companies, are exempt from federal
BOI reporting. Do not file a federal BOI report for ADA unless the rule changes
or counsel says otherwise. Banks may still request beneficial-owner information
for account opening.

## CPA Decisions Needed

- Confirm disregarded-entity treatment for 2026 and no S-Corp election.
- Decide bookkeeping method and chart of accounts.
- Decide simplified vs actual home-office method.
- Review asset transfer method: owner contribution vs reimbursement/purchase.
- Review FMV evidence for existing computers, peripherals, and robot.
- Decide depreciation/Section 179/bonus depreciation treatment.
- Review software/IP transfer value and amortization/expense treatment.
- Confirm handling of AI/API subscriptions paid personally before ADA banking.
- Confirm whether any local business tax receipt or registration is required
  before local revenue begins.
